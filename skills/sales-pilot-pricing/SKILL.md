# B2B SaaS Pilot Pricing Strategy

Structure and price pilot programs that convert to paid customers. Designed for enterprise deals, channel partners, and high-value prospects.

## When to Use

- Propose a pilot to a new enterprise prospect
- Negotiate pilot terms and pricing
- Structure a proof-of-concept (POC) with clear success metrics
- Convert a pilot to paid subscription
- Create a channel/partnership pilot program

---

## Pilot Pricing Models

### Model 1: Free Pilot (Low Commitment)

**When to use:**
- Very early-stage product
- Prospect has high authority and can influence many others
- Market validation is more important than immediate revenue

**Structure:**
- 30-60 days free
- Limited to 1-2 use cases
- Success metrics clearly defined
- Automatic conversion to paid or expiration

**Risk:** Low commitment from prospect

---

### Model 2: Discounted Pilot (Recommended)

**When to use:**
- Standard enterprise sales motion
- Prospect needs to justify investment internally
- Want to ensure skin in the game

**Pricing formula:**
```
Pilot Price = 5-15% of Expected Annual Contract Value (ACV)
```

**Examples:**
| Expected ACV | Pilot Price (10%) | Duration |
|--------------|-------------------|----------|
| CHF 10,000 | CHF 1,000 | 30 days |
| CHF 50,000 | CHF 5,000 | 60 days |
| CHF 100,000 | CHF 10,000 | 90 days |

---

### Model 3: Revenue-Share Pilot

**When to use:**
- Enterprise deal with uncertain ROI
- Customer wants to pay based on results
- High-trust relationship

**Structure:**
- No upfront fee
- Take 5-15% of attributed revenue during pilot
- Requires tracking infrastructure

---

### Model 4: Channel/Partner Pilot

**When to use:**
- Partner will onboard their customers
- Platform integration play
- Want to prove concept before full rollout

**Structure:**
- Free or heavily discounted
- Focus on partner's flagship customer
- Success = partner endorsement/reference

## Key Pilot Terms

### Duration
| Type | Duration | Best For |
|------|----------|----------|
| Quick pilot | 30 days | Fast decision makers |
| Standard pilot | 60 days | Most enterprise deals |
| Complex pilot | 90 days | Large organizations, multiple stakeholders |

### Success Metrics (Pick ONE)

| Metric | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| **Conversions recovered** | % increase in tracked conversions |
| **CPA reduction** | % decrease in cost per acquisition |
| **Revenue attributed** | CHF amount of tracked revenue |
| **Compliance achieved** | CMP implemented, consent flow working |
| **Data visibility** | % of conversions now visible |

### The Give-Get

```
PROSPECT GETS:
- Full access to [PRODUCT] for [DURATION]
- [X] hours of implementation support
- [X] hours of training
- Weekly check-in calls
- Success metrics report

PROSPECTOR PAYS:
- CHF [AMOUNT] (non-refundable)
- Commits to [X] hours/week internal champion time
- Provides access to [tools/data]
- Agrees to 1-hour final presentation

THIS OFFER EXPIRES: [DATE + 30 days]
```

## The Pilot-to-Paid Conversion

### During Pilot: Build the Case

1. **Week 1:** Document current state (baseline metrics)
2. **Week 2:** Show early wins (even small improvements)
3. **Week 3:** Quantify value with data
4. **Final week:** Present ROI calculation

### The Conversion Conversation

**Script:**
> "The pilot data shows [X]% improvement in [metric]. If we extrapolate to a full year, that's approximately [CHF amount] in [value]. Based on this, I'd recommend moving to our [TIER] plan at [CHF/year]. This includes [features]. Shall I send over the contract?"

### Pilot Extension Criteria

If they're not ready to convert:
- Is there a budget cycle issue? → Offer to extend pilot to next quarter
- Is there a stakeholder gap? → Invite additional decision makers
- Is there a technical blocker? → Resolve in extended pilot
- Is there no fit? → Thank them, ask for referral

---

## Enterprise-Specific Tactics

### Handling Procurement

1. **Start with business terms, not legal** — "Let's agree on value first, then my legal team will work with yours"
2. **Pre-agree on success metrics** — Get sign-off before pilot starts
3. **Offer a "pilot extension"** — If they need more time, make it conditional on progress

### Common Objections

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We want a free pilot" | "We can do free for 14 days to prove it works, but 30+ days requires investment to ensure you're committed to actually using it" |
| "Pilot too expensive" | "What's the expected ACV? The pilot is [X]% of that. If we don't deliver value, you walk away with [X]% risk" |
| "Need longer to decide" | "Let's extend but with clear milestones. What needs to happen by [date] for you to commit?" |

## Key Rules

1. **Always charge something** — Free pilots have 40% lower conversion rates
2. **Time-box strictly** — No open-ended pilots
3. **One success metric** — Don't measure everything, measure ONE thing well
4. **Get commitment before pilot** — What's the criteria for moving to paid?
5. **Expire the offer** — Creates urgency, shows confidence
6. **Build the case DURING the pilot** — Don't wait until the end
